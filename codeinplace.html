Mazes

Karel, in any size maze world, pick up all the beepers and put it down in a pink corner, functioning as a scoreboard.
The second has worlds with vertical walls, Karel find the finish line. Karel must pass through the gaps of them until it reached the line.

Random Watermelons Painting

She paints randomly black corners at the beginning. Those must be separated by at least 16 corners in width and 9 in height. So, it checks and removes the corners that doesn't allow to paint a full watermelon.
Then, Karel moves in the world searching black corners. When she finds one, she paints a watermelon.

Automaton

Karel following Conway's rule 22.
The initial state is chose randomly between the simple initial condition (one corner paint in the midpoint of the last street) or multiple painted corners randomly separated. This works in any size word, so it finds automatically the last row and mindpoint.
Living cells are blue and dead cells are blank.
